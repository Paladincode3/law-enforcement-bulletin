Supreme Court Watch (2020-2026)

2024-2025 Term (Recent & Pending)

Barnes v. Felix

Use of Force / Moment of Threat

Issue: Whether the Fourth Amendment analysis of an officer's use of deadly force is limited strictly to the "moment of the threat," or if the officer's pre-seizure conduct can be considered.

View Docket

Ames v. Ohio Dept. of Youth Services

Title VII / Reverse Discrimination

Issue: Clarifies the burden of proof for majority-group plaintiffs (reverse discrimination) in employment discrimination claims.

View Docket

Garland v. VanDerStok

Firearms / "Ghost Guns"

Issue: Addressed the ATF's authority to regulate firearm parts kits (frames and receivers) as "firearms" under the Gun Control Act of 1968.

View Docket

Chiaverini v. City of Napoleon

Malicious Prosecution

Holding: Even if probable cause exists for one charge, a Fourth Amendment malicious prosecution claim can still proceed regarding other charges that lacked probable cause.

Key Precedent (2020-2023)

United States v. Rahimi (2024)

Firearms / Domestic Violence

Holding: Confirmed that individuals subject to domestic violence restraining orders can be temporarily disarmed consistent with the Second Amendment.

Read Opinion (PDF)

Vega v. Tekoh (2022)

Miranda Rights / Civil Liability

Holding: A violation of Miranda rules does not provide a basis for a ยง1983 civil rights lawsuit. Miranda is a prophylactic rule, not a constitutional right for civil liability purposes.

N.Y. State Rifle & Pistol v. Bruen (2022)

Firearms / Concealed Carry

Holding: Struck down "may issue" concealed carry regimes. Established that gun regulations must be consistent with the nation's "historical tradition."

Read Opinion (PDF)

Torres v. Madrid (2021)

Use of Force / Seizure Definition

Holding: The application of physical force to the body of a person with intent to restrain is a "seizure" even if the person does not submit and escapes.

Read Opinion (PDF)

Lange v. California (2021)

Warrantless Entry / Hot Pursuit

Holding: Pursuit of a fleeing misdemeanor suspect does not categorically justify warrantless entry into a home. Officers must demonstrate exigent circumstances.

Read Opinion (PDF)

Caniglia v. Strom (2021)

Community Caretaking Exception

Holding: The "community caretaking" exception is limited to vehicles and does not justify warrantless entry into a private home to seize weapons for mental health reasons.

Read Opinion (PDF)

Kansas v. Glover (2020)

Traffic Stops / Reasonable Suspicion

Holding: An investigative stop is reasonable based on a revoked license check of the registered owner, provided the officer lacks information negating that the owner is the driver.
