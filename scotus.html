Decisions Issued (Jan 2026)

Case v. Montana

Topic: Warrantless Entry / Emergency Aid Doctrine
No. 24-624 DECIDED: JAN 14, 2026
Facts: Police responded to a call regarding a suicidal subject. Officers entered the home without a warrant to render aid. The defendant argued police needed "probable cause" to believe a crime was occurring or aid was needed.

The Ruling: The Court held (Unanimous) that police do not need probable cause to enter a home for emergency aid. They only need an "objectively reasonable basis" to believe an occupant is seriously injured or threatened with injury.
LE TAKEAWAY: The "Emergency Aid" exception is distinct from criminal investigation. If you have reasonable facts suggesting someone is hurt or dying inside, you may enter to help. You do not need the higher standard of probable cause required for a criminal raid.

Certiorari Granted (Upcoming Cases)

Chatrie v. United States

Topic: "Geofence" Warrants & Digital Privacy
No. 24-118 GRANTED: JAN 16, 2026
The Issue: Whether "Geofence Warrants" (ordering Google/Apple to identify everyone in a geographic area during a crime) violate the Fourth Amendment prohibition against "General Warrants."

Why it Matters: This decision will determine if investigators can continue using location-dump warrants to solve crimes where there are no specific suspects yet.
STATUS: Argument likely Spring 2026. Prepare for potential changes to digital warrant applications.
