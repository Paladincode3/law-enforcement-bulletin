Supreme Court Watch (2020-2026)

A curated list of U.S. Supreme Court decisions directly impacting law enforcement officers, first responders, and criminal investigators. Cases are categorized by term year.

Docket 23-1239 • Decided 2025

Barnes v. Felix

Use of Force / Moment of Threat

Issue: Whether the Fourth Amendment analysis of an officer's use of deadly force is limited strictly to the "moment of the threat," or if the officer's pre-seizure conduct can be considered in the totality of circumstances.

Docket 23-1039 • Decided 2025

Ames v. Ohio Dept. of Youth Services

Title VII / Reverse Discrimination

Issue: Clarifies the burden of proof for majority-group plaintiffs (reverse discrimination) in employment discrimination claims, relevant for department HR and promotion disputes.

Docket 23-852 • Decided 2025

Garland v. VanDerStok

Firearms / "Ghost Guns"

Issue: Addressed the ATF's authority to regulate firearm parts kits (frames and receivers) as "firearms" under the Gun Control Act of 1968.

Docket 23-50 • Decided 2024

Chiaverini v. City of Napoleon, Ohio

Malicious Prosecution / 4th Amendment

Holding: Even if probable cause exists for one charge, a valid Fourth Amendment malicious prosecution claim can still proceed regarding other charges that lacked probable cause.

Key Decisions (2020-2023)
Decided 2024

United States v. Rahimi

Firearms / Domestic Violence

Holding: Confirmed that individuals subject to domestic violence restraining orders can be temporarily disarmed consistent with the Second Amendment.

Download Opinion (PDF)
Decided 2022

Vega v. Tekoh

Miranda Rights / Civil Liability

Holding: A violation of Miranda rules does not provide a basis for a §1983 civil rights lawsuit. Miranda is a prophylactic rule, not a constitutional right in itself for the purpose of civil liability.

Download Opinion (PDF)
Decided 2022

N.Y. State Rifle & Pistol Ass'n v. Bruen

Firearms / Concealed Carry

Holding: Struck down "may issue" concealed carry regimes. Established that gun regulations must be consistent with the nation's "historical tradition of firearm regulation."

Download Opinion (PDF)
Decided 2021

Torres v. Madrid

Use of Force / Seizure Definition

Holding: The application of physical force to the body of a person with intent to restrain is a "seizure" even if the person does not submit and escapes. (Shooting a fleeing suspect is a seizure).

Download Opinion (PDF)
Decided 2021

Lange v. California

Warrantless Entry / Hot Pursuit

Holding: Pursuit of a fleeing misdemeanor suspect does not categorically justify warrantless entry into a home. Officers must demonstrate exigent circumstances on a case-by-case basis.

Download Opinion (PDF)
Decided 2021

Caniglia v. Strom

4th Amendment / "Community Caretaking"

Holding: The "community caretaking" exception to the warrant requirement is limited to vehicles and does not justify warrantless entry into a private home to seize weapons for mental health reasons.

Download Opinion (PDF)
Decided 2020

Kansas v. Glover

Traffic Stops / Reasonable Suspicion

Holding: When an officer lacks information negating an inference that the owner is the driver of the vehicle, the investigative stop is reasonable based on a revoked license check of the registered owner.
