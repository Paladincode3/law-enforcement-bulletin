Officer Safety: Vehicle Search (Exigency)

JAN 14, 2025 Commonwealth v. Brown, 2025 PA Super 14

Holding: Priority Alert. The Superior Court has ruled that the "exigency" required for a warrantless vehicle search ends the moment a suspect is secured in handcuffs or a patrol car. Officers MUST obtain a warrant once the scene is safe.

Read 2025 Opinion

Legislation & Case Law: Forced Abandonment

DEC 12, 2024 Commonwealth v. Joyner, 2024 PA Super 289

Topic: Flight & "Forced Abandonment"

New Ruling: While Matos (2003) is the general rule, this recent decision clarifies that unprovoked flight PLUS the observation of a "heavy object" in a pocket in a high-crime area constitutes Reasonable Suspicion. Therefore, the gun discarded during the chase was ADMISSIBLE.

Accountability & Authorized Actions

FEB 02, 2025 Commonwealth v. Tuthill, 2025 PA Super 22

Topic: Out-of-Jurisdiction Authority (MPJA)

Impact: Confirms that officers traveling through neighboring towns on "official business" (e.g., transport, court, routine patrol route) maintain full authority to enforce traffic laws if they witness a violation posing a danger to persons or property.
